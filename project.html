Project Background & Origin

In the spring of 2026, a road trip to Big Bend National Park—a designated International Dark Sky Sanctuary—fundamentally shifted our understanding of the nocturnal environment.

While sitting by a campfire, we waited for the flames to completely die out to experience total darkness. The transition was profound. In the absence of local light interference, an ineffable celestial density became visible. The stars crowded the velvet sky with some little specs and others slightly larger. Some glittered and some seemed to form little shapes. You could see Sagittarius or Capricorn or envision your own figure. For the first time, we observed the true, uninhibited scale of the night sky.

The "Aha!" Moment: Returning home to North Texas, the contrast was jarring. The pristine, deep-space clarity of West Texas was replaced by a bleached-out urban skyglow. The constellations we mapped easily at Big Bend were entirely obscured by artificial twilight.

This stark disparity brought a question to mind: Why are we losing our night skies? Our subsequent search found 1 of several culprits: standard, unshielded cobra-head streetlights driving high volumes of wasted upward light spill. This notebook documents our investigation into a scalable, mechanical solution to reclaim that lost clarity.

Core Investigation Objective

We are systematically investigating whether a removable, low-cost mechanical retrofit can successfully reduce upward and lateral spill light from existing cobra-head fixtures without compromising civil roadway safety margins.

Engineering Design Constraints

Any proposed physical mechanism must adhere strictly to the following parameters to ensure viability for municipal deployment:

Constraint Parameter Technical Rationale
No Permanent Modifications Zero drilling, tapping, or altering of the underlying utility fixture to comply with municipal liability and warranty standards.
Low Added Mass Minimal weight footprint to prevent structural strain, weld fatigue, or changing the center of gravity on the existing mounting arm.
Rapid Deployment Easy, rapid installation and removal using basic hand tools to minimize city crew time required in bucket trucks.
Universal Interoperability Geometric compatibility with standard, widely deployed legacy cobra-head housing profiles.
Unimpeded Maintenance Zero interference with maintenance access points, internal electronics housing, or photocell functionality.
Photometric Compliance Total preservation of required down-light illuminance values on the driving surface to maintain public roadway safety.
