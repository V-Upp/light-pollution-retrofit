Isolated the legacy cobra-head profile as our target platform due to its widespread municipal use, and prioritized blue-light wavelength filtering.

Completed Actions
  • Chose the standard M-250R style cobra-head utility light as our baseline configuration.

Next Engineering Phase: Build an explicit problem statement balancing safety metrics against dark sky goals.

Project Scoping, Problem Framing & Primary Criteria Tracking

Began the investigation following field observations of light pollution variations between urban metroplex environments and West Texas dark-sky sanctuaries.

Historical phase logs include establishing criteria for non-destructive mounting mechanisms, evaluating baseline ecological impacts, and drafting initial tracking sheets.

Engineering Decision History

[LOG-DEC-01] Awaiting Simulation

Decision: Integrated Ventilation Windows

Reasoning Strategy: Subtracted four 25x75mm cutouts directly from the main casing shell to establish air transit channels and lower aerodynamic lift variables during high winds.

Trade-offs Evaluated: Open slots allow rainwater, dust accumulation, and debris inside, requiring a design validation check on component degradation over time.

[LOG-DEC-02] Iterating Baseline

Decision: Separate Amber Filter Sheet Shelf

Reasoning Strategy: Configured a separate 610x178x3mm slot framework beneath the refractor to allow routine cleaning and quick swapping without replacing the main structural frame.

Trade-offs Evaluated: Multiplies total physical tracking components, introducing mechanical vibration concerns at the retention clip points.

Systemic Lessons Learned & Structural Self-Critique

Maintaining analytical integrity requires documenting project limits and process corrections. The following adjustments have been implemented in our workflow:

  • Content Architecture Correction: Initial design frameworks were structured like marketing pitches. We stripped out general summaries in favor of quantitative engineering parameters and explicit mechanical constraints.
  • Simulation Requirements: We recognize that claims regarding glare reduction or wind survival mean nothing without data. We will not publish performance percentages until our model completes ray-tracing and CFD wind-tunnel testing.
  • Outreach Discipline: Building a physical model without expert feedback risks wasting time on non-compliant ideas. We are prioritizing direct communication with utility engineers and lighting professionals before finalizing any physical tooling plans.
