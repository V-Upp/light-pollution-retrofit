27" Cobra-Head Retrofit Hood — Version 2.0

Modular, low-profile, clamp-on retrofit engineered to reduce high-angle light and glare from legacy municipal cobra-head streetlights, without drilling or permanent modification.

Design Objectives

[01]

Reduce High-Angle Light & Glare

Block and redirect stray light projecting above the horizontal plane to cut skyglow and glare at the source.

[02]

Minimize Uplight & Backlight

Suppress the BUG rating components tied to wasted skyward and rear-facing emissions per IES classification standards.

[03]

No Drilling or Permanent Modification

Clamp system fits standard 1¼" to 2" OD tenon arms without altering the OEM fixture in any way.

[04]

Quick Installation & Removal

Four-step install sequence: hook rear of hood, swing up and position clamps, tighten evenly, complete.

[05]

Low Added Wind Load

Target of under 10% increase in projected area and drag force versus the baseline unshielded fixture.

[06]

Maintain Roadway Illumination

Preserve usable downward lux levels so DOT roadway uniformity compliance is not compromised.

  • Clamps fit standard 1-1/4" to 2" OD tenon arms.
  • Rubber pads prevent vibration and protect fixture finish.
  • Filter module slides in from the front and is removable.
  • All components are corrosion-resistant.

Technical Evidence (Preliminary)

Wind Load Estimate

Baseline Fixture: Projected area 0.37 m² · Drag force reference 96 N.

With Hood (V2.0): Projected area 0.40 m² (+8.1%) · Drag force 104 N.

Result: Estimated increase stays within the 10% target.

Optical Impact (DIALux Evo Simulation)

Avg. Roadway Illuminance: 21.3 → 20.5 lux (−3.8%).

Avg. Sidewalk Illuminance: 4.7 → 2.1 lux (−55%).

Backlight/Uplight/Glare (BUG): B2→B1, U2→U0, glare significantly reduced.

Result: Significant reduction in spill & glare with minimal roadway impact.

Thermal Considerations

Ventilation: Louvers provide ~1200 mm² of free area per side for convection.

CFD (simplified): Max steady-state temp rise on LED housing <3–4°C at 25°C ambient with 1 m/s wind.

Curved bottom sustains air gap for exhaust airflow.
