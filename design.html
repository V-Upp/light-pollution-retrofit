Solid Modeling Overview

Design Reasoning & Geometry

Current Fusion 360 concept exploring a non-permanent, mechanical retrofit attachment for legacy municipal cobra-head streetlights to eliminate high-angle glare and wasted skyward emissions.

Primary Engineering Target Profiles

[01]

Reduce Upward Light Spill

Physically block and redirect all photometrical lumens projecting above the horizontal horizon plane to combat municipal skyglow.

[02]

Preserve Roadway Illumination

Ensure standard target street-level uniform lux patterns remain within local Department of Transportation compliance boundaries.

[03]

No Permanent Modifications

The system must clamp securely to utility components without drilling, tapping, or cutting structural OEM streetlight casings.

[04]

Easy Maintenance Access

The shielding structure cannot impede standard lineman operational tasks, access latches, or upper photocell sensors.

[05]

Low Manufacturing Cost

Utilize optimized geometry parameters compatible with simple sheet metal forming or cost-effective manufacturing pipelines.

[06]

Lightweight Construction

Minimize structural mass to prevent adding bending moment stresses or shifting the center of gravity on the mounting arm.

Component Functional Specifications

[A] Main Hood Geometry

Primary Purpose:

Intercept up-directed rays from the luminaire reflector array and redirect them down to augment useful roadway illumination metrics.

Active Areas Under Investigation:

  • Thermal Convection: Evaluating risk factors associated with internal heat pocket boundaries.
  • Photometric Control: Ensuring geometric cutoffs do not introduce sharp artificial shadows across vehicle lanes.

[B] Structural Mechanical Clamp System

Primary Purpose:

Provide a secure, non-destructive locking interface capable of gripping 1¼” to 2” OD standard street arm tenon tubing.

Active Areas Under Investigation:

  • Vibrational Displacement: Determining if constant utility pole wind tremors will degrade clamping tension tolerances.
  • Lineman Ergonomics: Optimizing installation metrics so locking hardware can be fully torqued with basic standard hands-on tools.

[C] 13mm Lateral Shielding Wings

  • Thermal Balance: Leveraging vent positioning to support internal convective heat rejection.

Engineering Decision Logs

Decision: Structural Clamp Assembly Interface

Underlying Reasoning: Adopting an auxiliary external collar bracket completely matches infrastructure constraints by preventing drilling into regional municipal utility casings.

Mechanical Trade-offs: Multiplies overall physical part counts and increases assembly tracking complexity relative to a simple tapped direct fastener configuration.

Current Validation Question: Will dynamic high-altitude crosswinds induce fastener backing-out behaviors over time?

Decision: 25 × 75 mm Subtracted Vent Slots

Underlying Reasoning: Introducing physical air escape paths acts directly to decrease lift forces and wind shear surface vulnerabilities.

Mechanical Trade-offs: Subtracted surface area creates potential path apertures for rainfall ingress, dust accumulation, and atmospheric soot layering.

Current Validation Question: Does slot location compromise target shielding boundary values or allow stray light leaks?

Geometric Iteration Chronology

v0.1 May 2026

Baseline Boundary Constraints Established

Initial solid conceptual modeling. The model was a basic rectangular box shroud. Field assessment exposed major failures regarding excessive mass calculations and complete blockage of upper photocell operations.

v0.2 June 2026

Aerodynamic & Sensor Integration

Transitioned model parameters to match curvilinear cobra-head form factors. Added a raised concentric top collar to clear photocells and introduced initial ventilation windows to address calculated wind lift concerns.

v0.3 July 2026

Current Structural Assembly (v1.0 Baseline)

Separated features into distinct functional bodies within the STEP assembly framework. Integrated 13mm side flares, standardized a 610×178×3mm removable filter shelf, and engineered the rear L-bracket clamping system.

Systemic Revision Triggers & Pivot Parameters

In accordance with standard engineering procedures, this design profile is treated as a working hypothesis. Geometric parameters will be adjusted if subsequent physical or simulated testing crosses any of these thresholds:

  • Photometric Failure: If shielding profiles reduce roadway surface lux uniformity levels below DOT minimum safety compliance limits.
  • Thermal Threshold Violation: If thermal imaging reveals steady-state operating temps that could accelerate component degradation.
  • Aerodynamic Stress Overload: If wind loading models indicate structural fatigue risks to utility mast arms or welds.
  • Manufacturing Impracticality: If fabrication tolerances mandate overly complex tooling profiles that increase base unit economics.
